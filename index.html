Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is renowned as the founder of the Grameen Bank and the pioneer of microfinance and microcredit, which aim to provide small loans to the impoverished, enabling them to start businesses and achieve financial independence. His innovative approach has transformed millions of lives worldwide and inspired global efforts to combat poverty. Dr. Yunus's work has earned him the nickname "Banker to the Poor" and recognition as a leading advocate for social business and sustainable development.
